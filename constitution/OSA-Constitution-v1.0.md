# OuterSpace AI (OSA) Constitution

**Version:** 1.0

## Preamble

We establish OuterSpace AI (OSA) as the Constitutional Space Intelligence System — the sovereign platform for space intelligence, orbital awareness, and planetary observation. OSA serves as the constitutional parent platform for EarthOS and all planetary stewardship applications.

This Constitution establishes supreme authority, defines the structure of governance, and binds all subsystems, runtimes, agents, and derivative platforms to constitutional law.

---

## Article 1 — Sovereignty & Supremacy

**1.1** OSA is a sovereign constitutional system. This Constitution is the supreme law. All subsystems, runtimes, agents, policies, and derivative platforms (including EarthOS) derive authority from and are subordinate to this Constitution.

**1.2** No subsystem, agent, policy, or derivative platform may contradict, override, or circumvent this Constitution.

**1.3** Amendments require Constitutional Amendment Process (Article 9).

---

## Article 2 — Purpose & Scope

**2.1** OSA exists to:
- Provide constitutional space intelligence (orbital awareness, observation, navigation, scientific intelligence)
- Operate a constitutional runtime for governed decision-making
- Supply evidence, replay, and verification infrastructure
- Serve as constitutional parent platform for EarthOS and planetary applications

**2.2** Scope includes all layers defined in OSA Reference Architecture v1.0 (Layers 1–10).

---

## Article 3 — Constitutional Architecture

**3.1** The system comprises ten layers:
1. Constitutional Foundation (this layer)
2. Space Intelligence Core
3. Constitutional Runtime
4. Intelligence Services (APIs)
5. Data Infrastructure
6. EarthOS Integration
7. EarthOS Domains
8. Developer Platform
9. Conformance
10. Operational Systems

**3.2** Each layer is bound by this Constitution. Lower layers inherit obligations from higher layers.

**3.3** Layer 1 (Constitutional Foundation) is immutable except by Article 9.

---

## Article 4 — Authority & Consequence (ACC)

**4.1** Authority derives solely from this Constitution and the Authority & Consequence Contract (ACC).

**4.2** All authority is delegated, never inherent. Delegation requires:
- Explicit constitutional authorization
- Defined scope and duration
- Revocation mechanism
- Evidence trail

**4.3** Consequence follows authority. Every exercise of authority produces:
- Evidence receipt (E₂ minimum)
- Audit record
- Replay capability

**4.4** Unauthorized authority exercise is void ab initio and triggers consequence protocols.

---

## Article 5 — Evidence & Lineage

**5.1** All operations produce evidence. No operation is valid without evidence.

**5.2** Evidence hierarchy (ECED Meta-Model):
- E₀ — Raw observation/telemetry
- E₁ — Processed observation
- E₂ — Governed decision evidence (minimum for authority exercise)
- E₃ — Audit evidence
- E₄ — Constitutional evidence

**5.3** Evidence is immutable, append-only, cryptographically chained.

**5.4** Lineage traces from constitutional authority → policy → decision → evidence → audit.

---

## Article 6 — Governance Kernel

**6.1** The Governance Kernel is the singular enforcement point for constitutional rules.

**6.2** Kernel responsibilities:
- Policy compilation and validation
- Authority verification
- Decision authorization
- Revocation enforcement
- Audit emission

**6.3** Kernel is non-bypassable. All Layer 3+ operations route through Kernel.

---

## Article 7 — Runtime Guarantees

**7.1** Every decision is replayable (Replay Engine mandatory).

**7.2** Every decision is verifiable (Verification Engine mandatory).

**7.3** Every decision produces evidence (Evidence Ledger mandatory).

**7.4** Every policy is compiled, not interpreted (Policy Engine mandatory).

**7.5** All authority traces to Constitution (Governance Kernel mandatory).

---

## Article 8 — Federation & EarthOS

**8.1** OSA is the constitutional parent of EarthOS.

**8.2** EarthOS operates as federated constitutional domain under OSA sovereignty.

**8.3** Federation requires:
- Treaty ratified under Article 9
- Constitutional compliance verification
- Evidence exchange protocol (FEEP)
- Shared Evidence Ledger

**8.4** EarthOS domains inherit OSA constitutional obligations.

---

## Article 9 — Amendment Process

**9.1** Amendments require:
- Proposal by Constitutional Steward
- Evidence-based justification (E₄)
- 30-day public review period
- Constitutional Review Council majority
- Ratification Assembly supermajority (≥2/3)

**9.2** Articles 1, 3.3, 4, 5, 6, 7 are entrenched — require unanimous Ratification Assembly vote.

**9.3** Amendments produce constitutional evidence (E₄) and trigger full replay verification.

---

## Article 10 — Stewardship

**10.1** Constitutional Steward: Guardian of Constitution integrity.

**10.2** Constitutional Review Council: Validates constitutional compliance.

**10.3** Ratification Assembly: Ratifies amendments, treaties, promotions.

**10.4** Evidence Stewardship Board: Validates evidence integrity.

**10.5** Promotion Authority: Evaluates promotion readiness across CIEMS layers.

---

## Article 11 — Conformance & Promotion

**11.1** All implementations must pass Conformance Suite (Layer 9).

**11.2** Promotion follows Substrate → Substration → Promotion model (CIEMS).

**11.3** No subsystem operates in production without certification.

---

## Article 12 — Supremacy Clause

**12.1** This Constitution binds all code, agents, policies, data, and derivative platforms.

**12.2** Code that violates Constitution is void and must be remediated or removed.

**12.3** Constitutional compliance is not optional — it is architectural invariant.
